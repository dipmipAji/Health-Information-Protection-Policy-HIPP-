Health Information Protection Policy (HIPP): A HIPAA-Aligned Framework
Author: Ajibawo, Oladipo Oluyemisi

Date: 17th March 2025


Overview
This article presents a Health Information Protection Policy (HIPP) developed to ensure compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). It covers all three HIPAA rules — Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification — and specifies administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for protecting Protected Health Information (PHI) and electronic PHI (ePHI).

Policy Purpose and Scope
Purpose: Provide a framework for protecting organisational information assets ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI, and compliance with HIPAA regulations.
Scope: All employees, contractors, patients, payers, and any individuals or entities who handle, process, or manage PHI. Covers all electronic, paper, and oral forms of PHI.
The Three HIPAA Rules Addressed:
Rule	Focus
Privacy Rule	Permitted use and disclosure of PHI; minimum necessary disclosure; individual rights
Security Rule	Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for ePHI
Breach Notification Rule	Notification obligations following unsecured PHI breaches


Administrative Safeguards
Standard	Key Requirements
Security Management Process	Risk analysis and management; sanction policy; activity review; assigned security responsibility
Workforce Security	Role-based access; authorisation and monitoring; termination procedures
Information Access Management	Authorisation controls; access limited to those who need it
Security Awareness and Training	Security reminders; malware protection; login monitoring; password management
Security Incident Procedures	Documented response to viruses, stolen credentials, backups, malware, physical breaches
Contingency Plan	Data backup; disaster recovery; emergency mode operations; regular testing
Evaluation	Annual review of security measures against HIPAA Security Rule


Physical Safeguards
Standard	Key Requirements
Facility Access Controls	Key card/biometric/controlled entry for facilities handling ePHI
Workstation Use	Documented workstation functions; clean-desk policy; time-out controls
Device and Media Controls	Disposal procedures; media re-use; accountability; data backup and storage


Technical Safeguards
Standard	Key Requirements
Access Controls	Unique user ID; emergency access; automatic log-off; encryption
Audit Controls	Hardware/software mechanisms to record and examine ePHI system activity
Integrity Controls	Electronic authentication to confirm ePHI has not been altered
Transmission Security	Encryption of ePHI in transit over electronic communication networks


Breach Notification and Response
1.	Breach Response Plan: Identification, containment, impact assessment, corrective measures
2.	Notification Timeline: Affected parties notified within 60 days of discovery
3.	Regulatory Reporting: HHS and (where required) media notification for large breaches; annual HHS reporting for breaches affecting fewer than 500 patients
4.	Post-Incident Review: Documentation of all response steps; root cause analysis; recurrence prevention

Responsibilities
Role	Responsibilities
All Employees	Adherence to policy; responsible data handling; incident reporting; mandatory training
IT Department	Adaptive security measures; system updates; compliance monitoring; incident management
CISO	Policy implementation and enforcement; risk management; incident coordination; security-business alignment


Definitions
Protected Health Information (PHI): Any Individually Identifiable Health Information in verbal, paper, or electronic form.
ePHI: PHI in electronic form.
HIPAA Privacy Rule: Standards for protection of individuals' healthcare information.
HIPAA Security Rule: Security policies for safeguarding ePHI.
Breach Notification Rule: Standards for reporting and notifying about PHI breaches.
